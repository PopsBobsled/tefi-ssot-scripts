# TEFI Direct Engagement Methodology
### Canonical Reference — Employment for Immigration NZ
*employmentforimmigration.nz*

---

## The Founding Principle

The voice connection is the starting line, not a step in a funnel.

Everything before it — researching the employer, identifying the decision-maker, finding a direct number — is preparation. It is necessary and it has value, but it does not register to the market. The market has not heard from you until a human being on the other end of a phone answers and listens to your pitch.

A standard online application is invisible. It joins hundreds of others in a queue. A voice-connected outreach followed by a personalised application is visible. The employer heard you, formed an impression, and will look at your profile differently when it arrives. That is the fundamental advantage of direct engagement. It cannot be replicated by any volume of emails alone.

Any response — including a clear no — is more valuable than silence. A no gives you market intelligence: sector demand, visa constraints, seniority expectations, local preference. A yes gives you a live opportunity. Both are outcomes. Both move the campaign forward.

## The Four KPIs

These are the only ratios that matter for measuring direct engagement performance. They are calculated from connected calls only — calls where a human answered. Non-connected attempts (voicemail, no answer, wrong number) are tracked for diagnostic purposes but are not included in performance denominators.

| KPI | Formula | Benchmark | What it measures |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Conversation rate** | Any response ÷ connected calls × 10 | 8 per 10 (builds over weeks, not days) | Pitch quality and professional presence. Most people give some response when approached directly and well. |
| **Positive rate** | Positive responses ÷ connected calls × 10 | Monitor — do not preset | Market demand signal. Varies by sector, role level, and immigration climate. Not a reflection of pitch quality alone. |
| **Follow-through rate** | Applications sent ÷ responses × 10 | 9–10 per 10 | Execution discipline. No warm contact should be left without an application. Near-perfect is expected. |
| **Connected calls / 5 days** | Count of Live Connected entries in last 5 calendar days | 10+ | Activity consistency. Below 10 per 5 days means gaps too wide for statistical learning or market momentum. |

**On the Positive Rate:** Sector benchmarks from TEFI practice —
- Teaching: approaches 10/10. Demand is structural and schools respond openly.
- Civil engineering, trades: approximately 8/10. Strong demand, direct communication culture.
- NFP / Charity: variable. Visa sponsorship walls are common. High block rate does not indicate poor performance — it indicates a structural sector constraint.
- Government / Ministry: often redirects to formal portals. Responses are valid but may not lead to direct applications.
- Health: mixed. Te Whatu Ora restructuring affects responsiveness. Individual DHBs or private providers respond well.

## What Counts as a Response

A response is any substantive reply received from an employer — verbal during the call, email the next day, or email two weeks later. Timing and channel do not change its validity.

Valid responses include:
- *"Yes, you could be considered — send me your CV."* — Positive. Application follows immediately.
- *"Sorry, we won't be taking skilled migrants for this role — we have enough locals at a high level."* — Acknowledged negative. Records market intelligence. Can open a request for advice or referral.
- *"I'll have a look and get back to you."* — Pending. Noted. Follow-up email sent at one week if no further contact.
- *"You'd need to apply through our HR portal."* — Redirect. Valid. Follow the instruction and note it.

All responses, regardless of type, are recorded in the Notes / Outcome column. No separate field is needed to classify them beyond the Response Received (Yes/No) indicator.

## Column Structure — Call Sheet (Tab 1)

**Research columns A–J** (filled before calling):

| Col | Field | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| A | Job link URL | Source listing — SEEK, LinkedIn, company site |
| B | Position title | As advertised |
| C | Employer name | |
| D | Sector / type | Used for sector benchmarking |
| E | Location / city | Used for regional analysis |
| F | Contact person name | Decision-maker or hiring manager — essential for direct call |
| G | Contact title | |
| H | Phone | Direct line preferred |
| I | Email | For application submission |
| J | Website | |

**Engagement columns K–P** (filled during and after calling):

| Col | Field | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| K | Call Date | Date | Date the call was made |
| L | Call Outcome | Dropdown | Live Connected / Voicemail / Not Connected / Wrong Number |
| M | Response Received | Dropdown | Yes / No — any response, any channel, any timing |
| N | Application Sent | Dropdown | Yes / No |
| O | Follow-up Date | Date | Date the 1-week nurture email was sent. Blank = not sent. |
| P | Notes / Outcome | Free text | Everything: call notes, employer messages, decisions, quotes |

**Column discipline:** One row per role per employer. If the same employer has two open roles, that is two rows. If a call to one role is not yet resolved, the row stays open — it does not become a new row.

## The Follow-up Protocol

One follow-up email. Sent one week after a connected call where no definitive reply has been received. The purpose is to demonstrate continuing genuine interest and to give the employer something of value — not to ask for anything.

**Tone:** Warm, brief, giving. Never chasing, never pressuring.

**What to give:** Something of real effort — a portfolio update, a project summary, a relevant article, evidence of recent work. Something that helps the employer picture the candidate in the role.

**Example:**

> Hi Phil,
>
> Just letting you know I am still keen for the role. I thought you might appreciate having my updated Photo Portfolio as a vetting tool, or to see what I have been doing in Construction Engineering, including several projects in Australia.
>
> Best regards, Carlo

If conversation rate before follow-up was 60%, this email typically recovers another 20% of responses. Record the date sent in column O. Any reply that comes back goes into column P (Notes / Outcome). No second follow-up is needed or recommended.

## Priority Action Framework

When reviewing a client's call sheet, assess in this order:

1. **No voice yet** — Phone numbers exist but zero Live Connected entries. The market has not heard from this candidate. Nothing else matters until this changes. *"Start calling."*

2. **Records incomplete** — Live Connected rows with no Response Received noted and no Notes. The conversation happened but wasn't captured. *"Update your call records — the data is invisible until it's written down."*

3. **Responses without applications** — Response Received = Yes but Application Sent = No. The employer signalled interest and the application has not arrived. This is the most urgent execution gap. *"X employers are waiting. Send the applications today."*

4. **Conversation rate low** — Fewer than 6 responses per 10 connected calls. Most people respond when approached well. Below 6 suggests the pitch opening needs adjustment. *"Refine how you open the conversation — most employers will engage if approached directly and briefly."*

5. **Activity below threshold** — Fewer than 10 Live Connected entries in the last 5 days. The campaign lacks the consistency needed for statistical learning. *"Increase call frequency — 10 connected calls per 5 days is the minimum for meaningful feedback."*

6. **Follow-up not deployed** — Connected calls more than 7 days old with no definitive response and no Follow-up Date recorded. *"The 1-week follow-up email hasn't been sent for X roles. This typically recovers 20% of pending responses."*

## Application to TEFI Programme Materials

This methodology is the reference point for:

- **Call sheet design** — columns, dropdowns, and validation rules reflect this logic exactly
- **Performance dashboard** — KPIs and benchmarks are drawn directly from this document
- **Call sheet analysis reports** — narrative sections (knowns, unknowns, questions, progress) are grounded in these four KPIs and the priority framework
- **Coaching emails to clients** — tone and content follow the principle that every response is both an opportunity and market research
- **Consultation sessions** — goal-setting conversations reference the 10 calls/5 days threshold and sector benchmarks as starting points, not prescriptions
- **Encouragement messages** — acknowledge effort at the preparation stage; celebrate voice connection as the real milestone; validate acknowledged negatives as learning events

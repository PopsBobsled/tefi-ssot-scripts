# 07d — Coaching Touchpoints

**Occasion:** Mid-engagement interventions — not stage milestones, not admin, not proposals.
**Who sends:** Tate — manually, when a specific client situation requires it.
**Primary modes:** Supportive Coaching + Consulting (varies by situation)

---

## What these are

Coaching touchpoints are situation-triggered emails sent during an active engagement when something specific has happened that warrants a direct response. They are not scheduled — they are written when the moment calls for them.

Unlike deal stage emails (which follow a fixed sequence) or admin emails (which handle logistics), coaching touchpoints respond to the client's *state* — their confidence, momentum, stalled progress, or a specific result like an interview booking or a rejection.

---

## The situations this folder covers

| Situation | Template type | Mode |
|-----------|---------------|------|
| Interview notes received — align with CV, build confidence | Interview alignment email | Consulting + Coaching |
| Client gone quiet during active engagement | Stalled client nudge | Coaching (warm or reset) |
| Client got no responses from applications | Stalled application redirect | Consulting |
| Client is anxious or overwhelmed | Anxiety normalisation | Supportive Coaching |
| Client re-engages after a long pause | Re-engagement without pressure | Consulting |
| Client received a rejection | Rejection reframe | Consulting + Coaching |
| Progress milestone worth acknowledging | Momentum acknowledgement | Consulting |
| Client fixated on a path that is not working | Objection reframe | Consulting |

---

## What this is NOT

This folder is not for:
- Stage milestone emails → those are in `07a-Deal-Stage`
- Proposal follow-up → that is in `07c-Proposal-Sequence`
- Scheduling or logistics → those are in `07e-Admin`
- Service offers → those are in `07f-Promotional`

---

## Key principle for this category

These emails require the most careful mode selection of any category. The client is in motion — they are in the program, doing the work — and a tone mismatch here is more costly than elsewhere. An anxious client getting a boundary-setting email, or a drifting client getting false reassurance, both cause damage.

Before writing any touchpoint email: identify the real situation (not just the surface request), select the primary mode from `01-Core-Reference/Mode-Selector.md`, then draft.
